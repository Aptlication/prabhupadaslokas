# Sloka Hub — Content Licensing Strategy
## BBT Permission Request & Executive Summary

## Executive Summary

*For internal review — prepared for leadership sign-off*

**The Situation**

Sloka Hub is a devotional mobile application designed to help ISKCON practitioners learn, understand, and memorise slokas from Srila Prabhupada's books. To fulfil its core purpose — searchable access to the full body of cited verses — the app requires access to the authoritative text of those works.

The most natural source for this content is **Vedabase** (vedabase.io), the BBT's own digital platform, which already houses the complete canon in a structured, searchable format.

**Why We Cannot Simply Connect to Vedabase**

Vedabase does not expose a public API. There is no developer portal, licensing endpoint, or documented access pathway for third-party applications. The only technically possible alternative — automated scraping of the website — is both prohibited under Vedabase's terms of service and legally precarious, as all content on the platform is protected under BBT copyright. Proceeding without authorisation would expose the project to legal challenge and would undermine its integrity as a devotional tool.

**Why Seeking BBT Permission Is the Most Elegant Solution**

Requesting a formal content licence from the BBT is not a workaround — it is the correct path, and the only one that is both sustainable and credible. Specifically:

1. **It is legally sound.** A licence from the rights holder removes all copyright risk, allowing the app to be published on the App Store and Google Play without legal exposure.

2. **It reflects the project's values.** An app built to honour Srila Prabhupada's teachings should be built with the same integrity those teachings embody. Formal permission aligns the method with the mission.

3. **It opens the door to an authoritative, living data source.** With BBT cooperation, the app can draw on Vedabase's full, maintained, and editorially verified text — rather than a static, manually curated dataset that will require ongoing upkeep.

4. **It is realistic.** The BBT has a documented history of supporting devotee-led digital projects. A non-commercial, educational, ISKCON-aligned application has strong grounds for approval.

5. **It positions Sloka Hub as an authorised platform.** This distinction matters for credibility within the community, for App Store categorisation, and for any future partnership or institutional endorsement.

The alternative approaches — manual curation of a limited dataset, or unauthorised scraping — are either insufficient for the catalogue feature or legally untenable. The BBT route is not the most cautious path; it is the most strategically sound one.

**Recommended Next Step**

Send the attached permission request to the BBT. If approved, the development team can integrate the licensed content in a structured, scalable way. While awaiting a response, the app will continue to be developed with the slokas already curated, so no momentum is lost.

I am writing to request permission to access and display content from the BBT's Vedabase platform within a mobile application called **Sloka Hub**, which we are developing for the ISKCON devotee community.

**What Sloka Hub Is**

Sloka Hub is a free, non-commercial mobile application designed to help ISKCON practitioners learn slokas from Srila Prabhupada's books. Its core features include:

- Searchable access to slokas with Devanagari script, transliteration, and translation
- Word-by-word meaning breakdowns (tap-to-reveal)
- Line-by-line pronunciation practice
- Personal collections and progress tracking (Learning / Learned)
- Planned integration with a full book catalogue for discovery by title and chapter

The app is devotional in purpose, ISKCON-aligned in ethos, and will be offered at no charge. It will be available on iOS and Android.

**What We Are Requesting**

We respectfully request:

1. **Permission to display sloka text** from Srila Prabhupada's works within the app, specifically: Bhagavad Gita As It Is, Srimad Bhagavatam, Sri Caitanya Caritamrta, Sri Isopanisad, Nectar of Instruction, Nectar of Devotion, and other works available on Vedabase.

2. **Access to Vedabase content via API or data feed** (if such a mechanism exists or can be arranged), so that the app can pull authorised, up-to-date content directly from the BBT's own platform rather than relying on a static copy.

3. **Guidance on attribution requirements** — we are fully committed to displaying BBT and Vedabase attribution in a prominent and respectful manner throughout the app.

**Why We Are Coming to You Directly**

We are aware that Vedabase does not offer a public API and that all content on the platform is BBT copyright. We have deliberately chosen not to pursue web scraping or any form of unauthorised access precisely because we want this application to reflect the same care and integrity that characterises Srila Prabhupada's own work. Doing this properly — with your blessing — is the only approach we are willing to take.

- Development stage: MVP complete; catalogue feature pending content licence
- Distribution: Free on App Store and Google Play
- Audience: ISKCON practitioners globally
